Step 1: Create a Twitch Developer Account

Visit the Twitch Developer Portal and click on the "Login with Twitch" button.

Step 2: Create a New Application

After signing in, click on "Your Console" located next to your avatar.

On the Developer Console page, click on "Applications" in the left sidebar and then click on the "Register Your Application" button.

Step 3: Fill in Application Details

Enter the necessary details for your application, such as the name, OAuth redirect URLs (use http://localhost), and category (select Application Integration). Then click on "Create."

Step 4: Obtain Client ID and Client Secret

Once your application is created, you'll be redirected to the application's settings page. Here, you'll find your Twitch Client ID. Click on the "New Secret" button to generate a Client Secret.

Step 5: You've Got Both the Client ID and Client Secret now!

Copy and paste the Twitch Client ID and Client Secret on the form above.

For more information, visit the Twitch Authentication Documentation.

Database
Step 4

Enter your Database settings

MongoDB Access URL:

Note:
 1. You need MongoDB for your bot to function properly.
2. If you don't have a MongoDB server, you can use MongoDB Atlas, which is a cloud MongoDB server provider. There is also a free plan you can use. Click on the button below for the guide.

Click here to show/hide the guide

Step 1: Sign up for MongoDB account.

Click on this link

Step 2: Confirm your email.

Check your email and click on the confirmation link. You'll then be asked for "Personalizing", just pick anything.

Step 3: Build a Database.

Click on the Build a Database button

Step 4: Select the M0 Plan (FREE)

Choose the M0 plan.

Select the Region. Make sure the region is the closest to you or your server. You can leave the Provider to AWS unless you have a specific need.

Enter the name for your Database. And finally, click the Create button

Step 5: Enter the Username and Password.

This is super important! Enter the username and password for your database. Save this somewhere safe. You'll need this later. Finally, click the Create User button.

Step 6: Add entries to your IP Access List

Enter 0.0.0.0/0 in the IP Address part, and Anywhere in the Description part.

Warning: It is not recommended to let ALL IP address access your database. If you know your IP address, you can enter it instead of leaving it to 0.0.0.0. You can find your IP address by going to this website. (Then you can enter like 123.456.789.012/32 into the IP address part)

Click Finish and Close

Step 7: Get the MongoDB Access URL

Click on the Database tab, and click on the Connect button on your Database.

Click the "Drivers" button

Copy the "connection string". Replace the <password> with your own password paste it into the MongoDB Access URL form above.

Info: If your password is 1234567 and your username is admin, then your connection string will be like this:
mongodb+srv://admin:1234567@databasename.something.mongodb.net/?retryWrites=true&w=majority
